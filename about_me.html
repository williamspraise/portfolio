If you were to solve one problem for a User, what would that be? 😀

The User

😜

I’m Williams, you can call me Liam, an African designer in Nigeria with dreams of working at Google. A UX Mentor in several UX Communities — but mostly I’m a UX enthusiast and a rapper.

Answer :

I have been in tech since 2014, I started off on HTML, moved into CSS, alongside Bootstrap, I multitasked that learning with graphic designing as at that time. I eventually migrated to Adobe Photoshop were I enhanced my skills on digital Imagery and Photography in the likes of Image retouching and Manipulation and Brand Design. I eventually transitioned into being a User Experience Designer, with my ability to solve the world's problem to the best I could as a creative individual. I have worked alongside WHOAfrica and Botswana in solving a problem for lack of identification of their health challenges to the world, seeking innovator's aid - I was a lead UX designer . 


Through my career of designing and managing, I’ve found that I’m at my best when I’m helping others succeed — today I do that through team project collaboration, speaking and mentoring. As a UX Designer with experiences in user research, empathy mapping, IA, rapid prototyping, inclusive design and several ideation methods, I have grown very intentional about solving the right problem

which is guided by an informed user research data, stakeholder's data and psychology to provide usable product solutions with avoidance of all forms of biases that are involved during this process. I seek the opportunity to solve more user problems around the world, and to give better user experiences and visual interaction to both stakeholders and end users as well as seeking the right platform for self-actualization as well. Features prioritization has become my compass to ensure MVP are met and scaled. I practice a specialist generalist, T-Shaped design with specialization in  Interaction Designs (IxD) and generalizes in UX.


In 2022 I founded Nxt Gen Xperience, a design firm set in place to equip newbies transitioning into tech with the right mindset, thougth process and knowledge on how they could drift through their path. During that time I learned to think and speak like a Developer which I was only that now it applies to design, honed my visual skills and came to understand the importance of business in design.


Before designing for a living, I was a programmer, beat producer and a rapper. I designed various engineering algorithms to aid students effeicacies and grades. My mind is never too far from the ocean, and I appreciate the importance of a healthy work/life balance. So when I’m not online, you’ll find me gaming on PlayStation, playing the Guitar, Writing Songs and beat making or hanging out with my loved ones.
